What is Testosterone Replacement Therapy?

Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT) is a medical treatment that supplements your body's natural testosterone when levels are clinically low. Unlike synthetic hormones, we use bioidentical hormones — molecules identical to those your body naturally produces — to safely restore optimal hormone levels.

TRT is specifically designed for men with hypogonadism (clinically low testosterone) who experience symptoms like fatigue, low libido, weight gain, muscle loss, and mood changes. Our personalized TRT programs are based on comprehensive blood panels and tailored to your unique physiology.

  • Increased energy and reduced fatigue
  • Improved mood and mental clarity
  • Enhanced libido and sexual function
  • Better sleep quality
  • Easier weight management
  • Improved body composition

Who is TRT For?

Testosterone Replacement Therapy is specifically designed for men with clinically low testosterone (hypogonadism). This condition is diagnosed through comprehensive blood testing and confirmed by experiencing multiple symptoms of low testosterone.

TRT may be right for you if you:

  • Have blood-tested low testosterone levels
  • Experience persistent fatigue or low energy
  • Have decreased libido or erectile dysfunction
  • Notice difficulty building or maintaining muscle
  • Struggle with mood changes or depression
  • Want to improve overall vitality and quality of life

Before starting TRT, we require comprehensive testing to ensure it's the right treatment for you and to establish a safe baseline for monitoring.

Medical Evaluation

Required Testing Before TRT

Before starting testosterone replacement therapy, we conduct comprehensive testing to ensure treatment is safe and appropriate for you. This thorough evaluation protects your health and establishes a baseline for monitoring.

Two Morning Testosterone Tests

Multiple early morning tests confirm clinically low testosterone levels for accurate diagnosis

FSH & LH Testing

Measures hormone signals from the brain to assess pituitary function and testosterone production

PSA Test (Men 40+)

Screens for prostate health to ensure TRT is safe and won't exacerbate underlying conditions

Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Evaluates red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets for overall health assessment

Comprehensive Metabolic Panel

Checks kidney function, liver function, blood sugar, and electrolyte levels

Additional Screening as Needed

May include thyroid function, estradiol, and other tests based on your symptoms and health history

Most common method, administered weekly or bi-weekly for consistent testosterone levels

Gels

Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions About TRT

What is testosterone replacement therapy?

Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT) is a medical treatment that supplements the body's natural testosterone when levels are clinically low. It's designed for men with hypogonadism or low testosterone, helping restore hormone levels to an optimal range for improved energy, mood, and overall vitality.

What testing is required before starting TRT?

Before starting TRT, we require two early morning testosterone tests to confirm low levels, along with testing for FSH, LH, PSA (for men over 40), CBC (complete blood count), and a comprehensive metabolic panel. This thorough testing ensures TRT is safe and appropriate for you.

What forms does TRT come in?

TRT is available in several forms including injections and topical creams. We also offer our natural Test Boost supplement for additional support. We work with you to determine which form best fits your lifestyle, preferences, and individual health needs.

How soon will I notice results from TRT?

Many patients report improvements in sleep quality and libido within the first 4 weeks of starting TRT. Full benefits, including increased energy and enhanced mood, typically become evident within 4-6 weeks of treatment.

What are the side effects of TRT?

Common side effects of TRT may include acne and temporary weight gain. More serious risks are rare when treatment is properly monitored. Our comprehensive monitoring protocols ensure we catch and address any issues early to keep your treatment safe and effective.

Does TRT cause prostate cancer?

No, research shows that TRT does not cause prostate cancer. However, it can potentially exacerbate pre-existing prostate cancer, which is why we screen all patients with PSA tests and prostate exams before starting treatment to ensure safety.

How long do I need to stay on TRT?

TRT is typically a long-term commitment. Once you start treatment, your body may reduce its own testosterone production. Stopping TRT will generally return your testosterone levels to their pre-treatment baseline. Most patients stay on TRT indefinitely to maintain the health benefits.
