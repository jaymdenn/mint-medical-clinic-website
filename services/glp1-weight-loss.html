What are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 (Glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonists like semaglutide (Ozempic®, Wegovy®) and tirzepatide (Mounjaro®, Zepbound®) are prescription medications that help regulate appetite and blood sugar. Originally developed for Type 2 diabetes, they've proven remarkably effective for chronic weight management.

These medications work by mimicking a natural hormone in your body that signals fullness to your brain, reduces appetite, and slows gastric emptying. This means you feel satisfied with smaller portions and experience fewer food cravings throughout the day.

Clinical trials have shown that GLP-1 medications can help patients achieve 10-20% body weight loss over several months when combined with lifestyle modifications. Tirzepatide trials demonstrated up to 20.9% weight loss at the highest doses, making it one of the most effective medical weight loss treatments available.

Common Questions

GLP-1 Weight Loss FAQs

What are GLP-1 medications?

GLP-1 medications are glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists originally developed for Type 2 diabetes management. They work by mimicking a natural hormone your body produces that regulates appetite and blood sugar. Brand-name medications like Wegovy and Zepbound are proven highly effective for chronic weight management, helping patients achieve sustainable, long-term weight loss.

How much weight can I expect to lose?

Clinical studies show patients typically lose 10-20% of their body weight over several months with GLP-1 medications. Tirzepatide clinical trials demonstrated up to 20.9% weight loss at the highest doses. For example, a person weighing 200 pounds could expect to lose 20-40 pounds. Individual results vary based on starting weight, lifestyle modifications, and adherence to the treatment protocol.

What are the side effects?

The most common side effects of GLP-1 medications include nausea, diarrhea, constipation, and abdominal pain. These side effects are usually mild and temporary, often subsiding within the first few weeks as your body adjusts to the medication. Our physician-supervised program includes personalized dosing that starts low and gradually increases, which helps minimize side effects while maximizing results.

Who qualifies for GLP-1 weight loss treatment?

GLP-1 medications like Wegovy and Zepbound are indicated for adults with a BMI of 30 or higher (obesity), or a BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related health condition such as high blood pressure, Type 2 diabetes, or high cholesterol. During your consultation, our physician will conduct a comprehensive medical evaluation to determine if GLP-1 therapy is appropriate for your specific health situation.

How are GLP-1 medications taken?

GLP-1 medications are administered via a once-weekly self-injection using a small, pre-filled pen device. The injection is simple and can be done at home in just seconds. You inject into fatty tissue areas like the belly, thigh, or upper arm. Our medical team provides thorough training on proper injection technique during your first visit, ensuring you feel comfortable and confident with the process.

How long do I need to stay on GLP-1 medications?

The World Health Organization (WHO) recommends long-term treatment for chronic weight management. GLP-1 medications work best when used as an ongoing therapy combined with lifestyle changes. Clinical studies show that weight may return if the medication is discontinued. Our program includes regular monitoring and support to help you maintain your results, with treatment duration personalized to your individual goals and health needs.

Is GLP-1 safe to use with other medications?

GLP-1 medications are generally safe, but it's essential that your physician reviews your complete medical history and current medications before starting treatment. During your consultation, we'll evaluate all medications you're taking, any existing health conditions, and potential drug interactions to ensure GLP-1 therapy is safe and appropriate for you. Our physician-supervised program includes ongoing monitoring for your safety throughout treatment.
