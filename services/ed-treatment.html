Understanding Erectile Dysfunction

Erectile dysfunction (ED) affects over 30 million men in the United States. If you're experiencing difficulty getting or maintaining an erection, you're not alone—and effective treatment is available.

ED isn't just about sexual performance. It's often an early warning sign of underlying health conditions like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, or hormonal imbalances. At Mint Medical Clinic, we take a comprehensive approach to find and treat the root causes, not just the symptoms.

Signs of Erectile Dysfunction

  • Difficulty achieving an erection
  • Difficulty maintaining an erection during sex
  • Reduced firmness compared to before
  • Decreased spontaneous erections
  • Reduced sexual desire or libido
Root Causes

What Causes Erectile Dysfunction?

ED is typically caused by one or more underlying factors. Understanding the cause is essential for effective treatment.

Vascular Issues

The most common cause of ED. Poor blood flow due to atherosclerosis, high blood pressure, or cardiovascular disease prevents adequate blood from reaching the penis.

Low Testosterone

Declining testosterone levels affect libido, arousal, and erectile function. Hormone imbalances can be diagnosed through blood testing and effectively treated.

Diabetes

High blood sugar damages blood vessels and nerves essential for erections. Men with diabetes are 2-3 times more likely to experience ED.

Psychological Factors

Stress, anxiety, depression, and relationship issues can contribute to ED. Performance anxiety creates a cycle that worsens the problem.

Medications

Blood pressure medications, antidepressants, antihistamines, and other drugs can cause ED as a side effect. Our team can review your medications.

Lifestyle Factors

Smoking, excessive alcohol, obesity, lack of exercise, and poor diet all contribute to ED. Lifestyle changes combined with treatment improve outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions About ED Treatment

What causes erectile dysfunction?

ED can be caused by physical factors (cardiovascular disease, diabetes, obesity, low testosterone, high blood pressure, nerve damage), psychological factors (stress, anxiety, depression, relationship issues), or lifestyle factors (smoking, excessive alcohol, lack of exercise). Often it's a combination of multiple factors.

How common is erectile dysfunction?

ED is very common, affecting approximately 30 million men in the United States. About 40% of men experience some degree of ED by age 40, and this percentage increases with age. However, ED is not a normal part of aging and can be treated at any age.

What ED treatments are available at Mint Medical Clinic?

We offer multiple treatment options including acoustic wave therapy (our flagship non-invasive treatment), oral medications, injectable therapies like TriMix, testosterone replacement therapy when low T is a factor, and the P-Shot (PRP therapy). Treatment is personalized based on your specific causes and goals.

Is acoustic wave therapy effective for ED?

Yes, acoustic wave therapy has shown significant effectiveness for ED, particularly for men with vascular causes. Clinical studies show 70-80% of patients experience improved erectile function. Unlike medications, it addresses the root cause by promoting new blood vessel growth.

How long do ED treatments take to work?

Oral medications work within 30-60 minutes. Acoustic wave therapy shows improvements within 2-3 weeks with optimal results at 3-6 months. Injectable therapies work within 5-15 minutes. Testosterone therapy improves sexual function within 3-6 weeks.
