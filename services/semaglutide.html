What is Semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a groundbreaking GLP-1 receptor agonist medication that has revolutionized medical weight loss. Originally developed for type 2 diabetes (Ozempic), it's now FDA-approved specifically for chronic weight management under the brand name Wegovy.

Unlike diet pills that force temporary appetite suppression, semaglutide works with your body's natural hormonal systems. It mimics GLP-1, a hormone that signals fullness to your brain, slows digestion, and improves how your body handles blood sugar.

How Semaglutide Works

  • Reduces appetite: Signals your brain to feel satisfied with less food
  • Slows digestion: Keeps you feeling full longer after meals
  • Reduces cravings: Helps control urges for high-calorie foods
  • Improves metabolism: Better insulin sensitivity and blood sugar control
  • Once-weekly injection: Convenient dosing with simple self-administration

Understanding Your Options

Ozempic vs. Wegovy: What's the Difference?

Both Ozempic and Wegovy contain semaglutide, but they're approved for different purposes:

Ozempic

  • FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes
  • Maximum dose: 2.0mg weekly
  • Weight loss is a secondary benefit

Wegovy

  • FDA-approved specifically for weight loss
  • Maximum dose: 2.4mg weekly (higher than Ozempic)
  • Includes FDA-approved cardiovascular benefits

At Mint Medical Clinic, we'll help you determine which formulation and dosing strategy is best for your goals.

What to Expect

Managing Side Effects

Most side effects are mild and temporary, especially with our gradual titration approach.

Common Side Effects

Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and stomach discomfort are most common when starting or increasing doses. These typically improve within a few weeks.

Our Mitigation Strategies

Gradual dose increases, dietary modifications, timing adjustments, and supportive medications when needed help most patients tolerate treatment well.

Serious Side Effects (Rare)

Pancreatitis and gallbladder problems are rare but serious. We monitor for warning signs and provide clear guidance on when to seek immediate care.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Semaglutide

What is semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist medication that mimics a natural hormone to reduce appetite and increase feelings of fullness. Brand names include Ozempic (for diabetes) and Wegovy (for weight loss). It's administered as a once-weekly injection and is FDA-approved for chronic weight management.

How much weight can I lose with semaglutide?

Clinical trials show patients lose an average of 15-20% of their body weight with semaglutide. Results vary based on starting weight, diet, exercise, and individual response. Most patients see significant results within 3-6 months of treatment.

What are the side effects of semaglutide?

Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and stomach pain, especially when starting or increasing doses. These typically improve over time. Gradual dose titration helps minimize side effects. Serious side effects are rare but include pancreatitis and gallbladder problems.

How long do I need to take semaglutide?

Semaglutide is typically used as a long-term treatment. Most patients maintain treatment for at least 12-24 months. Research shows weight regain often occurs after stopping medication, so ongoing treatment or a maintenance plan is usually recommended.

Is semaglutide covered by insurance?

Insurance coverage for semaglutide varies. Wegovy (weight loss indication) may have limited coverage. We offer affordable self-pay pricing and financing options through LiteCredit to make treatment accessible regardless of insurance status.

Who is a good candidate for semaglutide?

Semaglutide is FDA-approved for adults with BMI ≥30 (obesity) or BMI ≥27 with at least one weight-related condition (high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, high cholesterol). It's not recommended for those with personal or family history of medullary thyroid cancer or MEN 2 syndrome.

How is semaglutide different from other weight loss medications?

Semaglutide is more effective than previous weight loss medications, producing 15-20% weight loss compared to 5-10% with older drugs. It works on multiple pathways including appetite, satiety, and metabolism. The once-weekly dosing is also more convenient than daily medications.
