Understanding Premature Ejaculation

Premature ejaculation (PE) is the most common male sexual dysfunction, affecting approximately 30% of men at some point in their lives. It's defined as ejaculation that occurs sooner than desired, typically within 1-2 minutes of penetration, causing distress to one or both partners.

The good news is that PE is highly treatable. With the right approach, most men can significantly increase their time to ejaculation and improve sexual satisfaction for both themselves and their partners.

Signs of Premature Ejaculation

  • Ejaculation within 1-2 minutes of penetration
  • Inability to delay ejaculation during intercourse
  • Ejaculation before or shortly after penetration
  • Frustration, embarrassment, or avoidance of sex
  • Relationship strain due to sexual dissatisfaction

How We Treat Premature Ejaculation

SSRIs and other medications can significantly delay ejaculation. These may be taken daily for ongoing protection or on-demand before sexual activity.

  • No systemic side effects

Combination Therapy

For optimal results, we often combine medication with behavioral techniques and lifestyle modifications for a comprehensive approach.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About PE

What is premature ejaculation?

Premature ejaculation (PE) is when ejaculation occurs sooner than desired during sexual activity, typically within 1-2 minutes of penetration. It's the most common male sexual dysfunction, affecting approximately 30% of men at some point in their lives.

What causes premature ejaculation?

PE can be caused by psychological factors (anxiety, stress, relationship issues, early sexual experiences), biological factors (abnormal hormone levels, neurotransmitter imbalances, inflammation of the prostate), or a combination. Some men have lifelong PE, while others develop it later.

What treatments are available for PE?

Treatment options include oral medications (SSRIs, tramadol), topical numbing agents (lidocaine sprays/creams), behavioral techniques, and combination approaches. The most effective treatment often combines medication with behavioral strategies.

How effective is PE treatment?

Treatment is highly effective for most men. Medications can increase time to ejaculation by 2-4x or more. Most patients see significant improvement within a few weeks of starting treatment.

Is premature ejaculation treatment confidential?

Yes, all treatment at Mint Medical Clinic is 100% confidential and HIPAA compliant. We understand the sensitive nature of PE and provide discreet, professional care.

Can PE be cured permanently?

While some men achieve lasting improvement through behavioral techniques, most require ongoing treatment. The good news is that treatment is highly effective, and many men successfully manage PE long-term with minimal effort.

Do I need a prescription for PE medication?

Yes, effective PE medications require a prescription. During your consultation, we'll evaluate your situation and prescribe appropriate treatment that you can fill at your preferred pharmacy or through our clinic.
