Frequently Asked Questions

What are Ipamorelin and CJC-1295?

CJC-1295 is a growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog that signals the pituitary to release growth hormone over time. Ipamorelin is a growth hormone-releasing peptide (GHRP) that triggers GH release without affecting cortisol or prolactin.

Why use them together?

When prescribed together, these peptides produce a 3-5 fold increase in growth hormone release compared to using either alone. CJC-1295 extends GH pulse duration while Ipamorelin amplifies the release for synergistic effects.

How long until I see results?

Improved energy and sleep typically appear within 2-3 weeks. Noticeable body composition changes occur within 4-6 weeks. Full benefits are typically seen at 3-6 months.

Is this safer than HGH therapy?

Yes. These peptides are growth hormone secretagogues that stimulate your body's natural GH production rather than introducing external growth hormone. They work within your body's natural rhythms and may reduce side effect risks.

What are the side effects?

Common side effects are generally mild and may include injection site reactions, water retention, or tingling. Ipamorelin specifically minimizes side effects by not affecting cortisol or prolactin levels.

How are they administered?

Ipamorelin and CJC-1295 are typically administered via subcutaneous injection, often at bedtime to align with the body's natural growth hormone release patterns. Your physician will provide specific dosing instructions.
